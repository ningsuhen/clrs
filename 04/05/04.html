Exercise 4.5.4

Can the master method be applied to the recurrence $T(n) = 4T(n/2) + n^2\lg{n}$? Why or why not? Give an asymptotic upper bound for this recurrence.

With $a = 4, b = 2$, we have $f(n) = n^2\lg{n} \ne \mathcal{O}(n^{2-\epsilon}) \ne \Omega(n^{2-\epsilon})$, so no - we cannot apply the master method.

Let's guess $\Theta(n^2\lg^2{n})$:

$$ \begin{align} T(n) & \le 4T(n/2) + n^2\lg{n} \\ & \le 4c(n/2)^2\lg^2(n/2) + n^2\lg{n} \\ & \le cn^2\lg(n/2)\lg{n} - cn^2\lg(n/2)\lg{2} + n^2\lg{n} \\ & \le cn^2\lg^2{n} - cn^2\lg{n}\lg{2} - cn^2\lg(n/2) + n^2\lg{n} \\ & \le cn^2\lg^2{n} + (1 - c)n^2\lg{n} - cn^2\lg(n/2) & (c > 1) \\ & \le cn^2\lg^2{n} - cn^2\lg(n/2) \\ & \le cn^2\lg^2{n} \end{align} $$

Exercise 4.6-2 is the general case for this.
